Joaquim L. Viegas

Invited Assistant Professor

IDMEC, Instituto Superior Técnico, University of Lisbon, Portugal

Biography

Joaquim Viegas is an Invited Assistant Professor at Instuto Superior Técnico, University of Lisbon. He teaches in the Scientific Area of Control, Automation and Industrial Informatics at the Department of Mecahnical Engineering. Joaquim holds a PhD degree in Sustainable Energy Systems from the MIT Portugal Program. His thesis focused on the development of novel Data Science solutions for smart grids. Currently, he is doing research as part of a large Industry 4.0 project in the Chemical Industry, leading the team responsible for research and development of novel solutions in the fields of Data Science, Simulation, Optimization and Automation.

He is specially interested in:

  • Research and development in Optimization and Operations Research to solve industrial problems in fields such as Scheduling, Vehicle Routing and Cutting & Packing. Strong focus on metaheuristic approaches such as Genetic Algorithms and Variable Neighborhood Search.

  • Application of Machine Learning techniques to develop innovative solutions for Electricity Utilities, namely on the field of consumer segmentation, demand forecasting, detection of non-technical losses and fault prediction.

  • Research on novel Automation solutions and frameworks under Industry 4.0, focusing on the acquisition and processing of large volumes of sensor data.

Featured Publications

M Solas, N Cepeda, JL Viegas
September 2019 In 2019 IEEE PES Innovative Smart Grid Technologies Europe

Convolutional Neural Network for Short-term Wind Power Forecasting

Wind power generation is becoming increasingly relevant to the power supply system as it is clean and renewable. This paper proposes a novel methodology for short-term wind power forecasting, based on a convolutional neural network (CNN). In this work, we evaluate the CNN ability of predicting the wind power generation by comparing it to two benchmarking methods – ARIMA and gradient boosting machine (GBM). We prove that CNN is well suited for this purpose, outperforming the other tested techniques, specially when the prediction horizon is greater than 1-hour. Besides, this paper shows that additional features like meteorological forecasts provide fruitful information, powering the CNN performance.

JL Viegas, PR Esteves, SM Vieira
October 2018 In International Journal of Electrical Power & Energy Systems

Clustering-based novelty detection for identification of non-technical losses

The reduction of non-technical losses is a significant part of the total potential benefits resulting from implementations of the smart grid concept. This paper proposes a data-based method to detect sources of theft and other commercial losses. The proposed method works on the space of four different indicators of irregular consumption, enabling the easy interpretation of results. A use case based on real data is presented to evaluate the method. The threat model considers sixteen different possible types of changes in consumption pattern that result from non-technical losses, including attacks and defects present since the first day of metering.

JL Viegas, SM Vieira, R Melício, VMF Mendes, JMC Sousa
July 2016 In Energy

Classification of new electricity customers based on surveys and smart metering data

We propose a process for the classification of residential electricity customers. The current state of the art is extended by using a combination of smart metering and survey data and by using model-based feature selection. Firstly, the normalized representative consumption profiles of the population are derived through the clustering of data from households. Secondly, new customers are classified using survey data and a limited amount of smart metering data. Thirdly, model-based feature selection results explain the importance of the variables and which are the drivers of different consumption profiles.

CM Salgado, JL Viegas, CS Azevedo, MC Ferreira, SM Vieira, JMC Sousa
June 2016 In IEEE Transactions on Fuzzy Systems

Takagi-Sugeno fuzzy modeling using mixed fuzzy clustering

We propose the use of mixed fuzzy clustering (MFC) algorithm to derive Takagi-Sugeno (TS) fuzzy models. Mixed fuzzy clustering handles both time invariant and multivariate time variant features, allowing the user to control the weight of each component in the clustering process.
